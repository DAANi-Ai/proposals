Usage Rules
Midnight Navy is the foundation. Use it for headers, dark sections, and anywhere you want authority. Haya Blue is the signature colour. Use it sparingly for accents, buttons, and elements that need to draw the eye. Silver Mist provides sophistication and breathing room. Never use Haya Blue as a background colour for large areas. Never pair bright blue text on white. Keep it refined. Less colour, more impact.

04
Voice and Tone
Haya AI speaks with quiet confidence. The brand does not need to shout. It communicates expertise through clarity, precision, and restraint. Think private wealth advisor, not car salesman.
The brand sounds like
  • "We design systems that think ahead."
  • "Your operations, refined."
  • "Built for businesses that refuse to settle."
  • "Precision-crafted intelligence."
  • "Results that speak for themselves."
  • "Schedule a private consultation."
The brand never sounds like
  • "Supercharge your business with AI!"
  • "Limited time offer!"
  • "We are the best AI company."
  • "Revolutionise" / "game-changing"
  • "Don't miss out!"
  • "Click here to learn more!"
Suggested Taglines
"Precision in Automation.
Elegance in Intelligence."
"Intelligence, crafted."
"Your business. Elevated."
"Where ambition meets automation."

Logo Mark Note
Your existing HV geometric mark is strong and could be retained as a secondary icon or favicon. It reads as modern and technical. We would recommend pairing it alongside the new Haya AI wordmark, not replacing the wordmark with it. The mark works best at small sizes (favicon, app icon, social avatar) while the full wordmark leads on websites, documents, and marketing.

07
Photography and Imagery
The visual language should match the brand: clean, sophisticated, and intentional. No stock photos with fake smiles. No cluttered tech imagery.
Use
  • Clean architectural photography
  • Abstract light and shadow compositions
  • Minimal tech imagery (single devices, clean desks)
  • Blue and silver toned photography
  • Professional headshots with neutral backgrounds
  • Geometric patterns and gradients
Avoid
  • Generic stock photos of people pointing at screens
  • Robot imagery or sci-fi AI visuals
  • Cluttered, busy compositions
  • Neon or overly vibrant colours in photography
  • Clip art, cartoons, or illustrations
  • Overprocessed or heavily filtered images
