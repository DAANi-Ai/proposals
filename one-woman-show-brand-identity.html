01
Brand Story
"The AI industry is full of brilliant women who never got a seat at the table. So we built our own."
One Woman Show exists because the AI revolution should not be a boys' club. Women make up half the world's talent, half the world's ideas, and half the world's ambition, yet they remain vastly underrepresented in the spaces where artificial intelligence is being built, discussed, and deployed. This is not a support group. This is a launchpad. A space where women learn AI on their own terms, build businesses with real tools, and connect with a community that celebrates progress over perfection. The name says it all: you are the whole show. The strategy, the vision, the execution. AI is just the instrument. You are the performance.

02
Core Values
These are the beliefs that shape every piece of content, every interaction, and every decision. They are not marketing statements. They are promises.
01
Representation Matters
Women cannot aspire to what they cannot see. One Woman Show puts women at the centre of the AI conversation, not as a footnote, not as a diversity statistic, but as the main act.
02
Skills Over Credentials
You do not need a computer science degree to build with AI. You need curiosity, guidance, and a community that meets you where you are. We teach by doing, not by lecturing.
03
Community, Not Competition
There is enough room for every woman at this table. We celebrate each other's wins, share openly, and build bridges between industries, backgrounds, and experience levels.
04
Action Over Theory
Ideas are wonderful. Shipping is better. Every lesson, workshop, and resource is designed to produce a tangible result. You will not just learn about AI. You will build with it.

Usage Rules
Rich Plum is the foundation for dark sections, headers, and anything that needs weight. Amethyst is the signature colour, used for buttons, links, highlights, and moments of energy. Soft Lilac provides breathing room and warmth. Blush Rose is a supporting accent for callouts, quotes, and gentle emphasis. Use the Pearl and Whisper tones for backgrounds. Never use the full palette at once. Pick two or three colours per composition and let them breathe.

05
Voice and Tone
One Woman Show speaks like your smartest friend who happens to be an AI expert. Warm, direct, encouraging, and never condescending. The brand has conviction without arrogance. It celebrates without patronising. It teaches without lecturing.
The brand sounds like
  • "You already have what it takes. We just add the tools."
  • "Built by women. For women. No apologies."
  • "Your business, automated. Your time, reclaimed."
  • "The future is not waiting. Neither are we."
  • "Less scrolling. More building."
  • "Pull up a chair. We saved you a seat."
The brand never sounds like
  • "Girl boss!" / "Boss babe!" / "Slay queen!"
  • "Even women can learn AI!"
  • "Don't be intimidated by technology"
  • "Limited spots! Sign up NOW!"
  • "AI will replace your job unless..."
  • "For the non-technical woman"

Tone by Context
On social media, the tone is conversational, encouraging, and a little playful. In educational content, the tone shifts to patient, clear, and empowering. In sales or landing pages, the tone is confident and direct, leading with outcomes. In community spaces, the tone is warm, celebratory, and generous. The common thread across all contexts: respect the reader's intelligence, never talk down, and always make her feel like she belongs here.

Icon / Favicon
For small applications (favicon, app icon, social avatar), use a condensed mark: the letters "OWS" with the "W" in Amethyst, or a single stylised spotlight/stage light icon in the brand purple. The icon should feel recognisable at 16px and elegant at 512px. Keep it simple. One shape, one colour, instant recognition.

08
Photography and Imagery
The visual language should feel aspirational, real, and warm. Show women in action: building, leading, collaborating. Never staged, never stock-photo generic.
Use
  • Real women working at desks, on laptops, in meetings
  • Candid, natural-light photography
  • Diverse representation across age, ethnicity, and style
  • Soft purple and warm tones in post-production
  • Clean, minimal workspace environments
  • Abstract gradient art in brand colours for backgrounds
Avoid
  • Stock photos of women "being amazed" by a laptop
  • Pink-washed "women's product" aesthetics
  • Robot imagery, sci-fi visuals, or matrix-style code
  • All-male team photos or panels
  • Overly filtered or heavily retouched portraits
  • Clip art, cartoons, or childish illustrations
09
Who This Is For
One Woman Show serves women across a spectrum of experience, but shares a common thread: they are ambitious, curious, and ready to take action with AI.
A
The Business Owner
She runs a business and knows AI could help, but feels overwhelmed by the noise. She wants clear, practical guidance from someone who gets what running a business actually looks like.
B
The Career Switcher
She is curious about AI, possibly looking to pivot her career or add AI skills to her resume. She does not want a bootcamp full of jargon. She wants a community where she can learn at her own pace.
C
The Builder
She already has some technical comfort and wants to go deeper. She is looking for advanced workshops, accountability, and a network of women who are actually building, not just talking about it.
D
The Connector
She might not be building AI herself, but she wants to be in the room. She is a leader, a decision-maker, or an influencer who wants to understand AI well enough to shape it within her industry.
